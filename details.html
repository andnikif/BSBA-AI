Course Details & Learning Outcomes

Descriptions for new or significantly modified courses in the BAIA concentration, and the overall program learning objectives.

New & Modified Course Descriptions

BUSA 301 (Modified): Data Management & Visualization for AI

Building on data acquisition, preparation, and visualization, this course emphasizes techniques crucial for effective AI, including data quality assessment, feature engineering basics, and handling diverse data types for AI model training. Continues use of industry-standard tools.

BUSA 305 (Modified): Python for Business Analytics & AI

Extends foundational Python for analytics to include practical application of core AI/ML libraries. Students will learn to implement, interpret, and evaluate basic predictive models using Python for common business problems. (Prerequisite: QANT 201)

BUSA 315 (New): AI Fundamentals for Business

Provides a conceptual understanding of major AI domains (Machine Learning, NLP, etc.), their underlying principles (without deep math/coding), and their applicability to solving business problems. Focuses on identifying opportunities for AI and understanding its capabilities and limitations.

BUSA 415 (New): AI-Enhanced Business Analytics

Explores the integration of AI techniques with traditional business analytics methods across various domains (marketing, finance, etc.). Covers advanced predictive modeling, AI-driven forecasting, anomaly detection, and evaluating the business impact of AI-enhanced insights. (Prerequisites: BUSA 305, BUSA 315)

BUSA 420 (New): AI Strategy & Ethics for Business

Focuses on the managerial aspects of AI adoption. Topics include assessing AI readiness, developing AI strategies aligned with business goals, evaluating third-party AI solutions, managing implementation challenges, measuring ROI, and navigating the ethical landscape of AI, including bias, transparency, and accountability.

BUSI 460 (New Capstone): Applied AI & Analytics Project

A culminating experiential course where student teams tackle a complex business problem using a blend of traditional analytics and applied AI techniques learned throughout the program. Emphasizes problem definition, data analysis, AI tool application, solution development, and presenting strategic recommendations. (Prerequisites: BUSA 415, BUSA 420)

Program Learning Objectives

Upon completion of the BS-BAIA program, graduates will be able to:

  • Master Core Analytics: Apply fundamental statistical and quantitative techniques, manage data, and utilize tools (like Python) for descriptive, diagnostic, predictive, and prescriptive business analysis.
  • Understand AI Fundamentals: Explain core concepts, capabilities, and limitations of various AI technologies relevant to business.
  • Apply AI in Business Contexts: Identify opportunities to leverage AI and implement basic AI models to enhance business analysis and decision-making in areas like marketing, finance, and operations.
  • Develop AI-First Mindset: Instinctively evaluate how AI can augment or transform traditional analytical approaches to generate greater business value.
  • Evaluate AI Solutions: Critically assess the suitability, performance, and business value of different AI analytics solutions and vendors.
  • Implement AI Strategically: Contribute to the development and implementation of AI strategies within an organization, considering resource requirements and change management.
  • Uphold Ethical Standards: Analyze and address the ethical implications of AI and data analytics, including issues of bias, fairness, transparency, and privacy.
  • Communicate Effectively: Clearly communicate complex analytical findings and AI-driven insights to diverse business audiences, both orally and in writing.
